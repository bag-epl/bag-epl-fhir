The **Elektronische Plattform Leistungen (ePL)** is a major digital transformation project initiated by the **Federal Office of Public Health (FOPH)** in Switzerland to modernise how key healthcare benefit lists and processes are managed, published, and exchanged electronically. It forms part of the broader **DigiSanté programme** to enhance digitalisation across the Swiss healthcare system.

Official FOPH ePL project page: [https://www.bag.admin.ch/de/elektronische-plattform-leistungen-epl](https://www.bag.admin.ch/de/elektronische-plattform-leistungen-epl){:target="_blank"}

---

### Project Purpose and Scope

The **ePL platform** will replace existing, outdated technologies used to publish and manage Switzerland’s:

- **Specialities List (SL)** – Spezialitätenliste (SL)
- **Congenital Defects Specialities List** – Geburtsgebrechen‑Spezialitätenliste (GGSL)
- **Medicines and Devices List** – Mittel und Gegenständeliste (MiGeL)

The goal is to make these lists available in a **digital, structured, and interoperable form** – accessible both through public browsing and via secure electronic interfaces (APIs). The platform will support both **public access** to published list data and **protected workflows** for application submission, review, and communication with stakeholders.

---

### Digitalisation and Modernisation

#### **Public and Protected Areas**

- The **public area** will allow users to view and search published list data similar to the current web portals.  
- The **protected area** will enable regulated users (e.g., marketing authorisation holders) to submit applications, view review status, and interact directly with BAG within a controlled environment.

#### **APIs and Machine‑Readable Data**

External parties such as **health insurers** will be able to retrieve data via electronic interfaces (APIs), facilitating integration into billing, payment, and reporting systems.

#### **Modern Technology and Standards**

- The old system will be replaced with a modern digital platform that provides enhanced search and filtering capabilities, and a contemporary user experience.  
- Instead of static Excel or XML downloads, the ePL will offer **structured electronic data exchange** through APIs.  
- The API specifications are based on [**IDMP (Identification of Medicinal Products)**](idmp.html) and [**FHIR (Fast Healthcare Interoperability Resources)**](fhir.html) standards, aligning with international interoperability frameworks and enabling future integration with regulatory and clinical systems.

---

### Project Timeline

The ePL is being rolled out over several years:

- **2025:** Progressive launch begins with the triennial review process and publication of the SL on the new platform. 
- **mid 2026:** Full digitalisation of SL publication processes is expected to be completed. 
- **2026–2027:** The scope will expand further to include **MiGeL and the SL listing application process**.

---

### Benefits and User Groups

#### **For Marketing Authorisation Holders**

- Direct access to submit and track applications for inclusion in the SL and GGSL.  
- A unified platform enabling status tracking and communication with FOPH.  
- Planned ability to auto‑import product master data from **Swissmedic** to reduce manual data entry.

#### **For Health Insurers**

- Electronic access to structured list data that is relevant for billing and reimbursement.

#### **For Software Vendors and Developers**

- API access to structured and machine‑readable data (JSON/XML) based on modern interoperability standards (IDMP/FHIR), enabling advanced system integration.

---

### Relevance to Swiss Healthcare Digitalisation

The ePL project accelerates Switzerland’s shift from manual and document‑centric processes toward **digital, standards‑based data exchange**. It lays the groundwork for:

- **Structured medicinal product data publication and retrieval**  
- **Standardised application workflows**  
- **APIs aligned with international mechanisms such as IDMP/FHIR** that support interoperability across regulatory, clinical, and insurer systems.

The ePL is part of the broader **DigiSanté programme**, which seeks to modernise healthcare IT infrastructure and data processes nationwide.

---

### Summary

- The **Elektronische Plattform Leistungen (ePL)** is a major Swiss digital transformation project led by the **Federal Office of Public Health (FOPH)**.  
- It will provide a digital platform for publishing and managing key healthcare lists such as the **SL**, **GGSL**, and **MiGeL**.  
- The platform will offer both public data access and secure workflows via modern APIs.  
- API specifications are designed to follow **IDMP & FHIR standards**, ensuring structured, interoperable data exchange.  
- Progressive deployment started in 2025, with full implementation expected by 2027.
